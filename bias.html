Consider how these biases might shape your assessment of the candidate.

A candidate enters the room and your colleague leans in and quietly remarks, "This is the one from that overconfident Gen Z group I mentioned." Although the candidate greets you politely and shakes hands confidently, the comment seems to echo in your head; as they begin speaking, you start listening for signs of arrogance in their responses.

A few minutes in, the candidate falters slightly when asked about a situation involving customer interaction. That brief stumble colours your overall impression, making the earlier confidence seem rehearsed.

Later, they hesitate while trying to recall details from a recent group project. You assume this reflects a lack of preparation rather than a case of nerves.

Near the end, they respond thoughtfully to a complex question about conflict management, outlining a well-reasoned approach. This strong final response stands out in your memory. When they mention their interest in hiking, you feel an unexpected sense of rapport because you also enjoy weekend trail walks.
